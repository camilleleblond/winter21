The AIG $1 Million Grand Prix


Riders from all across the world compete for the chance to win money and noteriety!


In the AIG $1 Million Grand Prix, horse and rider are asked to navigate a course of 10 to 16 jumps up to 1.60m (about 5') in height and spread, in the fastest amount of time possible. Each jumpt that the horse knocks down translates to 4 faults. Course designers designate a specific "time allowed" for how long the riders should be able to complete the course, each second over the time allowed translates to 1 fault. The courses are difficult tests on both the riders and horses, requiring true performances of agility, quick-thinking, calmness, and ability. The goal of all riders is to complete the first course with 0 faults, allowing them to move on to the Jump Off. The Jump Off is a little shorter than the first course, but the course designers increase the difficulty for this round. The Jump Off is the true test, the rider who completes that course the fastest with the fewest faults is the winner!

Specifications


The top 40 rider & horse combinations, based on money won in other Grand Prix events, are invited to compete in the AIG $1 Million Grand Prix. Additionally, the horse show management has the ability to invite 5 additional riders to compete, if they wish to do so. The riders may ride only one horse, but may ride any horse of his or her choice.
